Fun Games Today!



Founded in the wild year of 2024, Fun Games Today! started as a scrappy bunch of friends with too much coffee, too little sleep, and one outrageous belief: that their games would someday be as addictive as their caffeine habits. The team is small, quirky, and dangerously obsessed with breaking gaming rules (because who even follows rules in games, right?). They began in a cramped apartment where their “office chairs” were thrift store beanbags, and their “meeting room” was just any corner that wasn’t already covered in pizza boxes.

Their first attempt at a game was supposed to be a “simple, classic” puzzle game… until they decided puzzles were boring and that adding exploding llamas, a talking cactus, and randomly generated dance parties would make it way better. Did it work? Technically, yes — if you ask their moms.

From there, Fun Games Today! committed themselves to creating games that entertain by surprising players with the unexpected. They ditched the mainstream, aiming instead to hit the sweet spot where “this makes no sense” and “I can’t stop playing” intersect. If you think a game’s plot should make sense, or that every level should be passable… this is not the studio for you. They aim to make games as hilariously chaotic as a toddler hopped up on candy.

Today, they’re pushing boundaries from a slightly larger (but still pizza-laden) “office” and still breaking out the beanbags when inspiration hits. They’re a mix of oddballs and geniuses, coding, designing, and testing games with the main goal to make you laugh, scratch your head, and lose track of what day it is. Play our games if you’re ready to embrace the absurd and enjoy a laugh along the way. After all, at Fun Games Today!, we don’t just make games — we create memorable chaos.

The Rules:

Each gesture can defeat two others and be defeated by two others. Here's how it works:

  1. Rock:
    • Crushes Scissors;
    • Crushes Lizard;
    • Paper covers Rock;
    • Spock vaporizes Rock;

  2. Paper:
    • Covers Rock;
    • Disproves Spock;
    • Scissors cut Paper;
    • Lizard eats Paper;

  3. Scissor:
    • Cuts Paper;
    • Decapitates Lizard;
    • Rock crushes Scissors;
    • Spock smashes Scissors;

  4. Lizard:
    • Eats Paper;
    • Poisons Spock;
    • Rock crushes Lizard;
    • Scissors decapitate Lizard;

  5. Spock:
    • Vaporizes Rock;
    • Smashes Scissors;
    • Paper disproves Spock;
    • Lizard poisons Spock;
Game Flow:
  1. A match is composed of 3 games sets;

  2. Each game set, the player chooses one of the five options: Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, or Spock;

  3. Player and Computer reveal their choices at the same time;

  4. The winner of the game set is determined based on the above interactions. If both players choose the same option, it's a tie;

  5. The match is based on the best of 3: So each game win is added 1; game loss is subtracted 1 point; and a tie adds no points;

  6. After the 3 game sets, the player is considered a "Match Winner" if player has 1 point or more; "Match Looser" if player has negative points; and a "Tie" if the player has 0 poinits.
